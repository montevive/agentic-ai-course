# LLM Agents as Heterogeneous Players: Agent-Based Modeling Meets Game Theory

- **Authors:** Novak, J.; El-Sayed, H.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Keywords:** agent-based modeling, game theory, heterogeneous agents, emergence

## Abstract

We embed LLM-driven agents with heterogeneous personas and strategies into classical game-theoretic environments, including iterated prisoner dilemmas, public goods games, and negotiation protocols. Unlike scripted agents, LLM players exhibit context-sensitive strategy shifts and natural-language signaling. Coordinated via a graph-based orchestration framework with per-agent memory of past interactions, populations of 50 agents reproduce known equilibrium behaviors while also displaying emergent reputation effects. We discuss implications for computational social science and mechanism design research.
