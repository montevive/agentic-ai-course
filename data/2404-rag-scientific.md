# Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Scientific Literature Review: A Reproducibility Study

- **Authors:** Fernández-Ruiz, E.; Hoffmann, B.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Keywords:** RAG, literature review, reproducibility, embeddings, evaluation

## Abstract

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) grounds language model outputs in external corpora, reducing hallucination in literature review tasks. We reproduce five published RAG pipelines over a corpus of 12,000 biomedical abstracts and measure citation accuracy, coverage, and factual consistency. Chunk size and embedding model choice explain 61 percent of the variance in citation accuracy, while reranking contributes marginal gains. We release an open evaluation harness so that research groups can audit RAG systems before deploying them as research assistants.
