# Coordination Patterns in LLM-Based Multi-Agent Systems: Sequential, Hierarchical and Consensual

- **Authors:** García-Pérez, M.; Silva, T.; Nkemelu, C.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Keywords:** multi-agent systems, coordination, supervisor, consensus

## Abstract

We formalize three coordination patterns for teams of LLM agents. Sequential pipelines pass artifacts through specialized stages; hierarchical topologies use a supervisor that delegates and integrates; consensual schemes require agreement among peer agents before committing results. On a benchmark of collaborative writing, data analysis, and literature review tasks, hierarchical coordination wins on quality (+18 percent over single agent) while sequential wins on cost. Consensus-based coordination only pays off when individual agent error rates exceed 25 percent.
